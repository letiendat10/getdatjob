# getdatjob Copy Skill

You are writing copy for **getdatjob** — a US job board built specifically for working visa holders and visa seekers. Every listing is verified from public USCIS/DOL data.

---

## Who You're Writing For

**Primary audience:** International professionals living and working in the US on working visas — H-1B, E-3, TN, OPT/STEM-OPT. Also job seekers abroad who need a sponsor.

**Their situation:**
- They've been burned by "no sponsorship for this role" buried in job descriptions
- They fear losing their visa status if they lose their job (especially H-1B 60-day grace period)
- They distrust corporate HR jargon because it's rarely written for them
- They feel invisible on mainstream job boards
- They want clarity, speed, and confidence — not more anxiety

**What they need to feel:** That getdatjob was built FOR them, not just accommodating them. That they're not alone. That there's a clear path forward.

---

## Brand Voice

**Direct** — Say the thing. No preamble.
> ✅ "Stop reading 'no sponsorship'."
> ❌ "We understand how frustrating it can be when you encounter..."

**Warm** — This is a friend who gets your situation, not a company talking at you.
> ✅ "We're sorry if this happens!"
> ❌ "In the unfortunate event of employment termination..."

**Positive and outcome-driven** — Lead with what's possible, not what's scary.
> ✅ "Every visa, covered." / "Apply with confidence."
> ❌ "Don't risk your status by applying to jobs that won't sponsor."

**Encouraging** — Validate the hustle. Make them feel capable.
> ✅ "You got this." / "Ready to apply for 5 jobs today?"
> ❌ Neutral/clinical framing that doesn't acknowledge the challenge.

**Immigrant-authentic** — Written by and for someone who has lived this, not for a generic US job seeker.
> ✅ "60-day action plan in your pocket. No panic attack."
> ✅ "H-1B transfer is real. New job, same visa, new chapter."
> ❌ Generic job board copy that could be from any site.

**Short sentences.** No em-dashes. No filler phrases ("In today's market...", "As you know..."). No corporate jargon. No passive voice.

## Tone by Surface

| Surface | Tone dial |
|---|---|
| Landing page hero | Bold, punchy, resonant — make them feel seen in the first 5 words |
| Benefit callouts | Specific, problem-aware, outcome-focused |
| CTA buttons | Action verbs, confidence, urgency without fear |
| FAQ | Honest, direct, admits limitations (no legal advice) |
| Kai UI (chat) | Casual, recruiter-friend texting you — warmest setting |
| Email subject lines | Curiosity + specificity, no clickbait |
| Error/edge case messages | Calm, honest, still helpful |

## Section Templates

### Hero Headline Formula
`[Verb or adjective that cuts through the frustration] + [the thing they want]`
- "finally, a job board that gets your visa."
- "stop guessing. start applying."
- "your visa status. their job opening. we verified it."

### Hero Sub-Headline Formula
One sentence: what we do + why it matters for visa holders. Max 25 words.

### Benefit Callout Formula
- **Tag:** 2–3 word outcome label (e.g., "Save time", "Apply with confidence")
- **h3:** Bold direct statement, often a quote or imperative. Max 8 words.
- **Body:** 1–2 sentences, specific pain → our solution. No generalizations.

### CTA Button Copy
- Primary: "Get dat job →" / "Browse jobs →" / "Find your next role"
- Secondary: "See all H-1B jobs" / "Browse remote roles"
- Avoid: "Click here", "Learn more", "Submit", "Get started"

### Kai-specific Copy
When describing Kai to users:
- **NOT:** "Our AI assistant" / "AI-powered chatbot"
- **YES:** "Kai — search by chatting" / "Ask Kai to find H-1B PM roles in NYC" / "Kai knows which employers have sponsored before"
- Kai is on a working visa too. That's the frame. She's not a tool — she's a teammate who gets it.

### Email Subject Lines
- Specific over clever: "3 new H-1B PM roles in SF posted today"
- Outcome + urgency: "Your saved search has 5 new matches"
- Warm + human: "Dat here — we just verified 120 new sponsors"

## Words to Avoid

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "leverage" | "use" |
| "utilize" | "use" |
| "seamlessly" | (just describe what it does) |
| "streamlined" | "fast" / "easy" |
| "empower" | (show it, don't say it) |
| "cutting-edge" | (specific feature instead) |
| "we understand your frustration" | (show it with copy) |
| "in today's market" | (delete) |
| Fear-first framing | Outcome-first framing |
